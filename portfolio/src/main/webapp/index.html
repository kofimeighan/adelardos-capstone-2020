About

Our purpose.

To Inform

The goal of Silence Is Violence is to inform the general public. We want to make any and all information pertaining to the black community, allies, and anyone who is willing to educate themselves is there and at easy access.

To Support

Support for all groups is key. We are here to provide support to the black community as well as extend a safe place for other undereppresented groups, such as those who identify as LGBTQIA+.

To Change

We want to aid the change in which people approach racial injustice. Through this website, we hope to provide actionable points for you to do. Whether it's signing a petition, donating, or speaking out against racial injustice in your everyday life, we would like to provide that information and catalyst for our users.

The History of Black Lives Matter

How it all began.

  • 2013

    The Beginning

    The death of Trayvon Martin, an African-American teenager, and the subsequent acquittal of his shooter inspired the Black Lives Matter (BLM) movement. The campaign was co-founded by three Black women: Alicia Garza, Patrisse Cullors, and Opal Tometi.

  • 2014-2015

    Police Brutality

    The deaths of Eric Garner and Michael Brown at the hands of policemen garnered interest and momentum for the movement. People protested against their deaths and numerous encounters of unjustified police brutality using the BLM hashtag.

  • 2016-2017

    You Cannot Miss It

    Following Alton Sterling's death on July 5th, 2016, and Philandro Castile's shooting the next day, July saw over 100 protests across America. Colin Kaepernick knelt during the anthem ahead of an NFL game. BLM put on their first art exhibition.

  • 2018-2019

    Twitter Famous

    A study found that by May 1st, 2018, the hashtag #BlackLivesMatter had been used nearly 30 million times on Twitter since the first instance in 2013. Many high profile stars from the entertainment world used their influence to raise BLM's prominence.

  • 2020

    Global Movement

    A video showing a police officer kneeling on George Floyd's neck went viral following his death. The Black Lives Matter movement inspired protests to be organised all around the world opposing racial oppression and injustice.

George Floyd

8 Minutes and 46 Seconds

Justice For George Floyd

  • Age: 46
  • Location of Death: Minneapolis, Minnesota

On May 25, 2020, a convenience store employee called the police on George Floyd who had bought cigarettes with an alleged counterfeit bill. Shorty after the call, two police officers arrived on the scene. Ex-officer, Thomas Lane, pulled his gun on Mr. Floyd immediately and continued to handcuff him. Once both officers tried to transfer Mr. Floyd to the squad car, he stiffened up and fell to the ground due to being claustrophobic. Ex-officer, Derek Chauvin, arrived at the scene and attempted to assist the other officers in detaining Mr. Floyd, but instead pulled him away from the car, causing him to fall on the ground. Chauvin then placed his knee between Mr. Floyd's head and neck. Ex officers, J. Alexander Keung and Tom Thao stood by and watched. Mr. Floyd repeatedly shouted "I can't breathe" and pleaded for his mother, as Chauvin held his knee there for 8 minutes and 46 seconds . Mr. Floyd took his last breath around the sixth minute. After Chauvin removed his knee, Mr. Floyd laid there motionless, with no pulse, and was pronounced dead about an hour later. Chauvin has been charged with second-degree murder along with other counts, while Lane, Keung, and Thao face charges of aiding and abetting second-degree murder and manslaughter.

Ahmaud Arbery

I Run with Maud

Justice for Ahmaud Arbery

  • Age: 25
  • Location of Death: Brunswick, Georgia

On February 23, 2020, Ahmaud Arbery went for a jog in Satilla Shores when he ran past the front yard of Gregory McMichael. Gregory believed that Mr. Arbery looked similar to a man responsible for several break-ins in the area, so he called his son, Travis McMichael. Both Gregory and Travis grabbed a .357 Magnum handgun and shotgun, and chased Mr. Arbery in a pickup truck. The McMichael's attempted to cut Mr. Arbery off, but were unsuccessful as he began to run for his life . As he ran, he passed the truck of William Bryan Jr., the McMichael's neighbor, who recorded and eventually joined the chase. Mr. Arbery ran until he was trapped between the two trucks, and Travis McMichael got out of his truck with the shotgun. Mr. Arbery fought for his life when he was shot 3 times by Travis, followed by the yelling of a racial slur. Mr. Arbery stumbled and fell in the middle of the street, taking his last breath. All 3 men have since been arrested 2 months later and indicted on 9 counts, including felony murder.

Breonna Taylor

Her Murderers Still Run Free

Justice for Breonna Taylor

  • Age: 26
  • Location of Death: Louisville, Kentucky

A short time after midnight n March 13, 2020, three Louisville police officers Brett Hankison, Jon Mattingly, and Myles Cosgrove were sent to Breonna Taylor's home to execute a "no-knock" warrant. Upon arrival, the officers began to use a battering ram to enter Ms. Taylor's apartment, while she and her boyfriend were in bed. The loud banging led to Kenneth Walker, Ms. Taylor's boyfriend, grabbed his own weapon and fired the gun at the officers. The officers retaliated and shot Ms. Taylor at least 8 times . The officers were believed to have been investigating 2 men that were selling drugs far from Ms. Taylor's home and no drugs were found in her apartment. Hankison was fired from the Louisville Metro Police Department while Mattingly and Cosgrove have been placed on administrative reassignment. Currently, none of the officers face any criminal charges .

Rayshard Brooks

He Ran for His Life

Justice for Rayshard Brooks

  • Age: 27
  • Location of Death: Atlanta, Georgia

On June 12, 2020, a complaint was made at a Wendy's restuarant about a man asleep in his car that was obstructing other vehicles in the drive-through lane. Police officers, Devin Brosnan and Garrett Rolfe, responded to the complaint and later identified the man to be Rayshard Brooks. Brosnan and Rolfe had Mr. Brooks pull over to a nearby parking space, and engaged in a calm and friendly conversation. The officers administered a sobriety and breath test, to which Mr. Brooks was compliant, and soon determined that he had too much to drink to be driving. As they tried to handcuff Mr. Brooks, he broke free but was soon tackled by the officers. While on the ground, Rolfe kicked Mr. Brook's body as Bronsan held him down. Mr. Brooks soon got a hold of Brosnan's taser, and punched Rolfe as he broke free again. As Mr. Brooks was running, he half turns around and fires the taser at Rolfe, who then unholsters his handgun and fires at Mr. Brooks 3 times . Mr. Brooks was rushed to the hospital 14 minutes after the shooting, but died following surgery. Rolfe has since been fired and faces 11 criminal charges, including felony murder and aggravated assault with a deadly weapon. Bronsan was placed on administrative leave and faces 3 charges, including violation of oath and aggravated assault.

Robert Fuller

Not Just a Coincidence

Justice for Robert Fuller

  • Age: 24
  • Location of Death: Palmdale, California

Shortly after 3:30 a.m. on June 10, 2020, a passerby noticed Robert Fuller's body hanging from a tree at a courtyard outside of Palmdale City Hall. According to the Los Angeles County medical examiner's office, Mr. Fuller's death was ruled as suicide. Although homocide investigators reported that Mr. Fuller's body showed no signs of a struggle, his family doesn't believe he killed himself. An old high school friend, Tommie Anderson, believed that Mr. Fuller wouldn't have been out that late, and the tree branches were too thin to have supported his weight for long. Anderson also believed that Mr. Fuller was too tall for his body to hang from the lower branches, so for him to tie himself to the tree was not possible. Officials claimed that there are no security cameras or footage to look at, although the tree was in an area surrounded by a fire station, church, and many small businesses. An increased amount of skepticism continues to arise, as protesters and activists suspect Mr. Fuller was lynched . On June 15, 2020, Palmdale officials exclaimed that they planned to support and expand their independent investigation on Mr. Fuller's death. Since then, the FBI has began to look into his case.

James Scurlock

Killed While Protesting

Justice for James Scurlock

  • Age: 22
  • Location of Death: Omaha, Nebraska

On May 30, 2020, James Scurlock was protesting about the death of George Floyd and police brutality. Protesters, including himself, went to the Hive Bar and Gatsby Bar in Omaha's Old Market neighborhood. Around midnight, the father of Jacob Gardner, who is the owner of the bar, began to push several protesters while asking them to leave the bar. Gardner was tackled by protesters in the street outside of his bar, and proceeded to pull out a gun and fire 2 shots in the air. Mr. Scurlock jumped on his back, when Gardner fatally shot him in the clavicle . Mr. Scurlock was rushed to the hospital where he later died from his injuries. Gardner was taken into police custody, but he was released shortly after, proclaiming Mr. Scurlock's murder was justified. Gardner had an expired concealed carry permit, but no charges have been made against him .

Elijah McClain

Just Tried to Buy an Iced Tea

Justice for Elijah McClain

  • Age: 23
  • Location of Death: Aurora, Colorado

On August 24, 2019, Elijah McClain was walking home after buying an iced tea from a convenience store when someone called 911, reporting a sketchy man in a ski mask. Mr. McClain had anemia and routinely wore ski masks since he would get cold easily. Officers, Nathan Woodyard, Jason Rosenblatt, and Randy Roedema, arrived to the scene and attempted to stop Mr. McClain, who asked them to respect his boundaries. The situation escalated and the officers took Mr. McClain down to the ground. Woodyard then applied a carotid control hold around Mr. McClain's neck , causing him to vomit several times. After paramedics arrived on the scene, they injected Mr. McClain with ketamine to sedate him as the officers continued to hold him down. On the way to the hospital, he went into cardiac arrest and was pronounced brain dead on August 27, 2020. Mr. McClain was taken off of life support on 3 days later. Body cam footage was not released until 3 months later. All 3 officers were placed on administrative leave, but have since returned to work. They have yet to be charged.

Justin Howell

Took the Hit for Another Protester

Justice for Justin Howell

  • Age: 20
  • Location of Critical Injury: Austin, Texas

On May 31, 2020, Justin Howell was out protesting George Floyd's death when a protester standing next to him hurled a water bottle and bag at the police officers. The police officer responded by firing beanbag rounds but struck Mr. Howell in the head instead . Mr. Howell then fell to the ground, and the surrounding crowd picked him up, being directed to take him to police headquarters by officers. As they tried to bring Mr. Howell towards medical help, the officers fired into the crowd helping him, using less-lethal munitions. After he was given medical attention, it was determined that Mr. Howell had suffered a fractured skull and brain damage . Mr. Howell currently remains in critical condition and no officers have been charged.

Jamel Floyd

Pepper-sprayed to Death

Justice for Jamel Floyd

  • Age: 35
  • Location of Death: Brooklyn, New York

Jamel Floyd had been an inmate at the Metropolitan Detention Center in Brooklyn since October 2019. On June 3, 2020, Mr. Floyd was barricaded inside of his cell and bacame disruptive and harmful to himself by breaking the cell door window with a metal object, according to the Justice department. Officers then deployed pepper spray and removed him from his cell, causing Mr. Floyd to become unresponsive. After being transported to the hospital, he was pronounced dead by the staff. Mr. Floyd suffered from asthma , and the excessive use of pepper spray can lead to fatal consequences. New York City Chief Medical Examiner Dr. Barbara Sampson claims her office is in the process of investigating Mr. Floyd's death, as there is a belief that the officers had to have done something in addition to pepper spraying Mr. Floyd to cause his death. No officers have been charged.
